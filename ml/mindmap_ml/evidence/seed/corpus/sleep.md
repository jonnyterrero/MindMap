# Sleep and next-day symptoms

Shortened or irregular sleep is one of the most consistently reported antecedents of next-day
migraine attacks in headache diaries. Nights below roughly six hours, and large night-to-night
swings in sleep timing, tend to precede attacks more often than stable, adequate sleep. This is an
association observed across many people's logs, not a guarantee for any individual.

Reduced or fragmented sleep also tends to coincide with higher next-day anxiety and lower focus.
People frequently notice that a poor night is followed by a more on-edge, less productive day. The
effect is probabilistic and varies between individuals.

Catching up on sleep and keeping a regular sleep–wake schedule are commonly discussed self-management
strategies. These are general wellness patterns to consider and discuss with a qualified professional,
not medical instructions.
