# Common migraine trigger patterns

Drops in barometric pressure are a frequently reported environmental correlate of migraine for some
people. A falling-pressure day, especially combined with a poor night's sleep, often appears in logs
shortly before an attack. Sensitivity to weather varies widely between individuals.

Stress and the period immediately after stress ("let-down") are commonly logged around attacks, as are
dehydration and skipped meals. None of these is a universal cause; they are candidate patterns worth
noticing in one's own data.

Tracking suspected triggers over time can help a person and their clinician see which patterns recur
for them. The goal is informed discussion, not self-diagnosis or changing any treatment unprompted.
