# Mood, activity, and routines

Regular daytime activity, time spent outside, and consistent routines tend to coincide with steadier
mood in self-tracked data. Stretches of very low activity sometimes precede lower mood and lower
focus, though direction of effect is hard to establish from logs alone.

Medication and routine adherence often move together with day-to-day functioning. Gaps in a usual
routine can show up alongside dips in mood or sleep. These are correlations a person may find useful
to reflect on, not evidence that one caused the other.

Sudden increases in energy or activity with reduced need for sleep are patterns some people track
around mood elevation. Anything in this area is best reviewed with a qualified professional rather
than acted on alone.
